Mission Statement

The Mitchell S. Cutler Charitable Trust is a private foundation headquartered in Washington, DC, USA. Our foundation annually awards grants up to $50,000 (USD) to non-profit organizations located primarily (though not exclusively) in the Washington DC Metropolitan area. The mission of the foundation is to support US based organizations dedicated to families in need, homelessness, and educational opportunities. The foundation favors initiatives or specific projects focused on one of its core focus on housing for needy families and educational opportunities for children.

Applicants must be organizations are that are non-sectarian IRS 501(c)(3) with projects that are consistent with the foundation’s mission statement.

Meet the Board

Miriam Cutler

Miriam Cutler (Mimi) has more than 30 years experience as a commercial real estate developer. Prior to that, she was an attorney at the U.S Department of Justice and ran a consumer protection non-profit group. Currently she maintains her real estate interests and works as volunteer with SCORE, mentoring small business owners. In 2012 she established this charitable foundation in the name of Mitchell S. Cutler to help people in need, as Mitchell did during his lifetime.

Audrey Whittington

Audrey Whittington is currently the Vice President of Client Services for Move Inc. She has over 25 years in the real estate industry providing business to business solutions on an enterprise level. Currently, she is responsible for maintaining and extending the value of all aspects of ListHub’s data solutions to all segments of the real estate industry. She is also currently on the board and the Chair of the Development Committee for the Arlington Academy of Hope, an organization whose mission is to help children in rural Uganda reach their full potential..

Leslie Sgro

Leslie Sgro, of Springfield, Illinois serves in the elected office of Springfield Park Board President for seven consecutive terms. She has also worked as the Deputy Director of the Governor's Office of Management and Budget for the State of Illinois, overseeing the state capital budget. Leslie serves on several not for profit boards and is an active volunteer in the community. She has served as trustee of the Mitchell S. Cutler Foundation since its inception, helping to direct funds to organizations that reflect the organization's mission.
